Coffee is a beloved brewed beverage renowned for its distinctive aroma and rich flavor, derived from the seeds nestled within the "berries" of coffee plants. These plants thrive in over 70 countries, primarily clustered around the equatorial regions of Latin America, Southeast Asia, India, and Africa. Among the diverse coffee cultivars, two reign supreme: the highly esteemed Arabica and the robust Robusta. While Arabica boasts a refined taste, Robusta offers a more robust and resilient profile, making it less susceptible to coffee leaf rust, known scientifically as Hemileia vastatrix. However, its flavor tends to carry a sharper bitterness. Once the coffee berries ripen to perfection, skilled hands meticulously pick, process, and dry the beans, ensuring optimal quality. Remarkably, green (unroasted) coffee beans emerge as one of the most sought-after agricultural commodities globally, contributing significantly to international trade. This intricate journey, from cultivation to consumption, underscores coffee's unparalleled allure and enduring global appeal.
